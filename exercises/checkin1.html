Interesting Facts about Hairless Guinea Pigs (Skinny Pigs)

  • Hairless guinea pigs were discovered in the 1970's as a result of genetic mutation due to research experiments in dermatology.
  • Also called skinny pigs, they are used in studies of skin reactions and pigmintation because their skin works very similar to human skin.
  • Their unique look made them become a popular pets, they aren't completely hairless and have hair on their legs and noses.
  • Wikipedia

Interesting Facts about Chameleons

  • There are more than 200 species of chameleons, as well as 23 subspecies.
  • Chameleons don't change color to camoflage. That is a common misconception. Most chameleons are already colors of leaves, bark, branches and sand. They change colors due to stress or other strong emotions.
  • Chameleon's have panoramic vision, they can rotate each eye separately to focus on two different things at once.
  • Wikipedia

Interesting Facts about Axolotls

  • Axolotls can lay up to 1,000 eggs at a time, two weeks later the eggs hatch and the babies are left to defend for themselves as soon as they are born.
  • Axolotls have a massive DNA genome, 10 times larger than humans and over 32 billion individual DNA strands.
  • They are critically endangered in the wild due to habitat loss, polution, and invasive species. Wild axolotls can only be found in Lake Xochimilco
  • Wikipedia
